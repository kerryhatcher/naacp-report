# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Readers of a commissioned report on Georgia elections administration: NAACP
staff and chapter organizers, journalists, researchers, county election
officials, and engaged Georgia voters. They arrive to look up a specific
county board, compare turnout numbers, or read findings — a reference they
return to, not a feed they scroll. Many will read on phones; data tables and
long-form prose must both work small.

### Brand Personality
Rigorous, independent, quietly urgent. The site is investigative journalism:
it should feel like a serious editorial publication with a documentary
temperament — authoritative without being bureaucratic, designed without
being decorative. Three words: credible, incisive, composed.

### Aesthetic Direction
Modern investigative-journalism editorial (reference class: ProPublica, The
Marshall Project, The Trace) with a fully independent palette — deliberately
NOT NAACP navy/gold, and not a generic "dashboard" look. Warm paper and ink
neutrals, one assertive editorial accent used sparingly (rules, kickers,
links, active states). Serif display and reading faces paired with a
grotesque sans for wayfinding and data. Newspaper details: hairline rules,
small-caps kickers, generous reading measure, asymmetric layout. Light and
dark themes, following the system (`light-dark()` / `color-scheme`).
Anti-references: SaaS landing pages, glassmorphism, cyan-on-dark AI slop,
card grids of identical rounded rectangles.

### Design Principles
1. **The data is the drama** — typography and rules carry the design; color
   appears where it means something (links, active states, emphasis).
2. **Reading first** — long-form measure ~65ch, fluid type scale, tables
   legible at phone width.
3. **Newspaper bones, modern build** — hairline rules, kickers, and serif
   display executed with fluid clamp() sizing and container-aware layout.
4. **One accent, spent carefully** — a single editorial accent hue; if
   everything is highlighted, nothing is.
5. **Both themes are first-class** — every token defined via `light-dark()`;
   never a dark theme bolted on after.
